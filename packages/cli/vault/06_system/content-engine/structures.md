# Post Structures - Content Engine

A library of proven post formats to rotate through. The idea and the structure are separate — pick an idea from the notes, then choose a structure that fits.

Last updated: YYYY-MM-DD

---

## X (Twitter) Structures

### 1. The Counterintuitive Truth
**Pattern:** Challenge a common belief, then reveal the real insight.
**Why it works:** Creates cognitive dissonance. People stop scrolling when their assumptions are challenged.

**Template:**
```
Most people think [common belief].

But [counterintuitive truth].

[One sentence that makes it land.]
```

**Best for:** Business strategy, industry takes

---

### 2. The Hard Truth List
**Pattern:** A short list of uncomfortable realities about a topic.
**Why it works:** High idea density. Each line is a mini-hook. Easy to screenshot and share.

**Template:**
```
Things no one tells you about [topic]:

- [Hard truth 1]
- [Hard truth 2]
- [Hard truth 3]
- [Hard truth 4]

[Closing line — either a question or a sharp conclusion]
```

**Best for:** Industry insights, lessons learned

---

### 3. The Observation
**Pattern:** A short, sharp insight. Just one idea, stated clearly.
**Why it works:** Reads like a thought from a smart friend. Low friction, high shareability.

**Template:**
```
[Observation in 1-2 sentences.]
```

**Best for:** All pillars. Great for testing new ideas.

---

### 4. The "We used to think / Now we know"
**Pattern:** Show how thinking evolved on a topic.
**Why it works:** Vulnerability + wisdom. Shows learning, not preaching.

**Template:**
```
We used to think [old belief].

After [experience/time], we realized [new belief].

[Why this matters or what changed.]
```

**Best for:** Transformation stories, personal growth

---

### 5. The Build-in-Public Update
**Pattern:** Share what was shipped, what was learned, or what's being worked on.
**Why it works:** Authenticity. People root for builders who show their work.

**Template:**
```
This week at @[YourCompany]:

[What we shipped / decided / learned]

Why it matters: [1-2 sentences on the impact]

[Optional: what's next]
```

**Best for:** Product updates

## LinkedIn Structures

### 6. The Personal Lesson
**Pattern:** Start with a personal moment, extract a universal lesson.
**Why it works:** LinkedIn rewards vulnerability and relatability.

**Template:**
```
[Hook — a specific moment or realization. Make it personal.]

[2-3 short paragraphs telling the story]

[The lesson — stated clearly]

[Closing thought or question to the reader]
```

**Best for:** Personal growth, leadership

---

### 7. The Framework Share
**Pattern:** Present a mental model or framework used to make decisions.
**Why it works:** Practical and saveable. People bookmark frameworks.

**Template:**
```
[Hook — the problem this framework solves]

Here's how we think about it:

[Framework in 3-5 points, each 1-2 sentences]

[How we've applied this / what it changed]
```

**Best for:** Business strategy, best practices

---

### 8. The "Here's what we're seeing"
**Pattern:** Share an industry observation with a take on what it means.
**Why it works:** Positions you as having a unique vantage point. Timely and relevant.

**Template:**
```
[Observation about something happening in the industry]

Here's what most people are missing:

[Interpretation / what it really means]

[What we're doing about it or what others should consider]
```

**Best for:** Industry transformation, product updates

## How to Use This Document

1. **Pick your idea** from the notes/idea bank
2. **Choose a structure** that fits the idea AND the platform
3. **Write the first draft** using the template as scaffolding
4. **Remove the scaffolding** — the final post should read naturally
5. **Cross-post consideration:** Write the X version first (most constrained), then expand for LinkedIn

### Structure Rotation Guide

To keep content fresh, avoid using the same structure more than twice in one week:

| Day | Suggested Structure Type |
|-----|------------------------|
| Mon | Observation or Counterintuitive Truth (X) |
| Tue | Personal Lesson (LinkedIn) |
| Wed | Hard Truth List or Framework (X + LinkedIn) |
| Thu | Product Update / Build in Public (both) |
| Fri | "Here's what we're seeing" or "We used to think" (both) |

## Adding New Structures

When you find a post you love (yours or someone else's), break it down:

1. What's the **hook type**? (question, bold claim, personal moment, data point)
2. What's the **body structure**? (list, narrative, framework, contrast)
3. What's the **close**? (question to reader, call to action, sharp one-liner)
4. Add it here as a new numbered structure with a template and example.
